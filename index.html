Out of the hive

When Sophie and her friends go out of the hive, they're going to eat. They're looking for flowers with nectar.
To eat, Sophie plunges its trunk inside the pistil of the flower and sucks the nectar. She then stores it in her jabot (it's a small pocket that is in her stomach).
The nectar that has been harvested is brought back to the hive where it's rumored by other bees. Thus, it mixes with saliva and turns into honey. This honey is then deposited in the cells of the hive. It will be used to feed the larvae (baby bees).

Pollen transport

The pollen of the flowers cling to the little hairs of the Sophie when she forage.
When she goes foraging another flower, she brings the pollen (the dad) of the flower to the pistillus of the next flower (the mother). The pistillus is the small stem inside the flower. Flowers contain pollen and a pistillus, but they need Sophie to meet from one to an other.

The growing flower

When Sophie put the pollen inside the pistillus, it will create a seed, which will fall and grow up to become a flower in turn.

The cycle

When the flower finished to grow up, the cycle start again. Sophie can pick up the pollen to bring him to the pistillus of another flower. Those flowers transform in fruits and vegetables. So without bees, we cannot eat.
You have to really take care of Sophie and her bees friends, they are precious for humans !

Sweety

To create honey, Sophie eat some flower's nectar, that she bring to the hive. Here, another bee take the nectar and put it in the hive at the right place. Than, guardian bees will take care of the honey until it is good to eat.
